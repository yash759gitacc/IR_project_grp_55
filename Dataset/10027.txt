Description
Jaigarh Fort, located on the Aravalli hills near Jaipur, Rajasthan, India, is a formidable fortress renowned for its massive walls, imposing gateways, and strategic location. Built-in the early 18th century by Maharaja Jai Singh II, the fort served as a defensive stronghold and treasury for the ruling Kachwaha Rajput dynasty. The fort's extensive ramparts and watchtowers offer panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, including the nearby Amer Fort and the city of Jaipur. Jaigarh Fort is also home to the world's largest cannon on wheels, Jaivana, which has never been fired in battle. Today, the fort is a popular tourist attraction and a reminder of Rajasthan's martial heritage.

Culture
Jaigarh Fort is deeply ingrained in the cultural and historical fabric of Rajasthan, serving as a symbol of the region's valor and resilience. It played a crucial role in defending the kingdom of Amber (now Jaipur) from external threats and invasions, earning a reputation as an impregnable fortress. The fort's architecture reflects a blend of Rajput and Mughal styles, with its sturdy battlements and palatial structures standing as testaments to the engineering prowess of its builders. Visitors to Jaigarh Fort can explore its sprawling complex, marvel at its architectural grandeur, and immerse themselves in the rich history and heritage of Rajasthan.

History
Jaigarh Fort was built in 1726 by Maharaja Jai Singh II, the founder of Jaipur, to protect the city and its treasures from potential attacks. Over the centuries, the fort underwent several expansions and renovations under successive rulers, including Maharaja Sawai Madho Singh II. It served as a strategic military outpost and a royal residence for the Kachwaha Rajput kings, who used it to safeguard their wealth and maintain control over the surrounding territories. Despite its formidable defenses, Jaigarh Fort never faced a major siege or battle and remained largely peaceful throughout its history. Today, it stands as a testament to Rajasthan's martial heritage and architectural splendor, attracting visitors with its panoramic views and historical significance.
