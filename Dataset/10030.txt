Description
The Great Living Chola Temples refer to a group of three Hindu temples in southern India that were built by the Chola dynasty during the 11th and 12th centuries CE. These temples, namely Brihadeeswarar Temple at Thanjavur, Airavatesvara Temple at Darasuram, and Brihadeeswarar Temple at Gangaikonda Cholapuram, are renowned for their architectural grandeur, exquisite sculptures, and intricate carvings. The Brihadeeswarar Temple at Thanjavur, in particular, is considered a masterpiece of Dravidian architecture and features a towering vimana (temple tower) that is one of the tallest in the world. The Great Living Chola Temples are UNESCO World Heritage Sites and are considered sacred pilgrimage destinations for Hindus.

Culture
The Great Living Chola Temples are an integral part of Tamil Nadu's cultural heritage and represent the zenith of Chola architectural and artistic achievements. These temples served as centers of worship, patronage, and royal authority during the Chola dynasty's rule. The temples are dedicated to Lord Shiva and are adorned with sculptures and reliefs depicting various Hindu deities, celestial beings, and mythological narratives. The intricate carvings and intricate details found in these temples reflect the rich cultural and religious traditions of ancient India, providing valuable insights into the socio-religious life of the Chola period.

History
The Great Living Chola Temples were built during the reign of the Chola emperors Rajaraja I, Rajendra I, and Kulothunga I, who were known for their patronage of art, architecture, and literature. Construction of these temples began in the 10th century and continued through the 12th century, spanning several generations of Chola rulers. The temples were built using granite stones and were adorned with elaborate sculptures and inscriptions that glorified the achievements of the Chola kings and celebrated their devotion to Lord Shiva. Despite facing natural calamities and invasions over the centuries, the Great Living Chola Temples have stood the test of time and continue to inspire awe and reverence among visitors for their architectural splendor and historical significance.
