Description
Bekal Fort, situated in Kasaragod district of Kerala, India, is an ancient coastal fortification overlooking the Arabian Sea. Built during the 17th century by the Nayakas of Keladi, and later captured and fortified by the rulers of the Ikkeri dynasty, the fort served as a strategic defense outpost against maritime invasions. Bekal Fort features massive laterite walls, bastions, watchtowers, and underground tunnels, showcasing the architectural ingenuity and military prowess of its builders. The fort offers panoramic views of the surrounding coastline and is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors with its historical significance and scenic beauty.

Culture
Bekal Fort is deeply rooted in the cultural heritage of Kerala, serving as a symbol of the region's maritime history and architectural legacy. The fort's strategic location along the Malabar coast made it a vital stronghold for defending against foreign incursions and safeguarding trade routes. Over the centuries, Bekal Fort has witnessed the rise and fall of empires, including the Vijayanagara, Nayaka, and Tipu Sultan's Mysore Sultanate. Today, the fort stands as a testament to Kerala's multicultural heritage, with its blend of Hindu, Muslim, and European influences evident in its architecture and historical significance.

History
The history of Bekal Fort dates back to the 17th century when it was constructed by the Nayakas of Keladi as part of their defense strategy against rival kingdoms and European colonial powers. The fort underwent significant expansions and renovations under the Ikkeri dynasty and later came under the control of the Mysore Sultanate. Bekal Fort played a crucial role in several historical battles, including the Mysorean invasion of Malabar and the Anglo-Mysore Wars. Despite its military significance, the fort eventually fell into disuse and was neglected until its restoration in the 20th century. Today, Bekal Fort stands as a proud symbol of Kerala's heritage and a popular tourist attraction, offering visitors a glimpse into the region's rich history and architectural splendor.
