Description
Agra Fort, also known as the Red Fort of Agra, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Agra, India. Constructed primarily of red sandstone, it served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1638 when the capital was shifted to Delhi. The fort complex encompasses a variety of architectural styles, including Islamic, Persian, and Hindu influences, reflecting the cultural richness of the Mughal Empire. Its massive walls, intricate palaces, and imposing gateways stand as a testament to the grandeur and power of the Mughal dynasty during its zenith.

Culture
Agra Fort is a cultural treasure trove that provides insights into the opulent lifestyle and architectural brilliance of the Mughal era. The fort served not only as a military stronghold but also as a center of governance, art, and culture. Its magnificent structures, such as the Jahangir Palace, Khas Mahal, and Diwan-i-Khas, showcase exquisite craftsmanship and artistic detailing. Today, Agra Fort continues to be a significant cultural heritage site, attracting visitors from around the world who marvel at its historical significance and architectural splendor.

History
Constructed by Emperor Akbar in 1565, Agra Fort has witnessed centuries of Mughal history, from the reign of Akbar to Shah Jahan and beyond. It underwent significant expansions and renovations under successive Mughal emperors, including Shah Jahan, who added several ornate structures and gardens to the complex. The fort played a pivotal role in the tumultuous events of Indian history, including the War of Succession and the imprisonment of Shah Jahan by his son Aurangzeb. Despite facing numerous sieges and battles, Agra Fort stands today as a symbol of resilience and architectural brilliance, preserving the rich heritage of the Mughal Empire.
