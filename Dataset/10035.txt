Description
Suraj Kund, located in the Aravalli hills of Faridabad, Haryana, India, is a historic reservoir and cultural complex known for its picturesque surroundings and vibrant festivals. Built during the 10th century by Raja Suraj Pal of Tomar dynasty, it is believed to have been a sun temple originally. Surrounded by lush greenery and rocky terrain, the reservoir is fed by an ancient spring and is considered sacred by locals. Suraj Kund hosts an annual crafts fair known as the Surajkund Mela, where artisans from across India showcase their traditional crafts, music, dance, and cuisine. The festival attracts thousands of visitors and is a celebration of India's rich cultural diversity.

Culture
Suraj Kund is steeped in cultural significance and folklore, with its origins dating back centuries. Legend has it that Raja Suraj Pal, the founder of the Tomar dynasty, built the reservoir to honor the sun god, thus giving rise to its name. Over the years, Suraj Kund has become a symbol of community spirit and cultural exchange, serving as a gathering place for locals and visitors alike. The annual Surajkund Mela showcases the diverse arts and crafts of India, providing a platform for artisans to showcase their talent and for visitors to experience the country's rich cultural heritage.

History
The history of Suraj Kund is intertwined with the ancient civilizations that once flourished in the region. Built during the Tomar dynasty's rule in the 10th century, the reservoir served as a source of water and a place of worship for the local populace. Over time, Suraj Kund gained prominence as a cultural and religious center, attracting pilgrims and devotees from far and wide. The tradition of hosting the Surajkund Mela began in 1987 with the aim of promoting traditional Indian arts and crafts and providing livelihood opportunities to artisans. Today, Suraj Kund stands as a testament to Haryana's rich cultural heritage and a symbol of India's vibrant cultural mosaic.
