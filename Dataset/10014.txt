Description
Baby Taj, formally known as the Tomb of Itimad-ud-Daulah, is a Mughal mausoleum located in Agra, India. Built between 1622 and 1628, it is often referred to as the "Baby Taj" due to its resemblance to the larger and more famous Taj Mahal. The mausoleum is made of white marble adorned with intricate inlay work, including delicate floral motifs and semi-precious gemstones. Its symmetrical design, with a central dome surrounded by four minarets, reflects the elegant architectural style characteristic of Mughal monuments.

Culture
Baby Taj is a significant cultural and architectural landmark that showcases the artistic brilliance of the Mughal Empire. It served as the tomb of Mirza Ghiyas Beg, a Persian nobleman and grandfather of Mumtaz Mahal, for whom the Taj Mahal was built. The mausoleum's exquisite craftsmanship and fine detailing make it a popular attraction for visitors interested in Mughal art and history. Baby Taj's serene gardens and riverside location add to its charm, creating a tranquil oasis where visitors can appreciate its beauty and historical significance.

History
Construction of Baby Taj was commissioned by Nur Jahan, the wife of Emperor Jahangir, in memory of her father, Mirza Ghiyas Beg. The mausoleum was designed by Persian architect Ustad Ahmad Lahauri, who also contributed to the construction of the Taj Mahal.
