Description
The Statue of Unity is a colossal statue located in the Indian state of Gujarat, near the Sardar Sarovar Dam on the Narmada River. Completed in 2018, it stands as a tribute to Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, one of the founding fathers of independent India and the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister of the country. Designed by Indian sculptor Ram V. Sutar, the statue depicts Sardar Patel in a standing posture, symbolizing his unwavering leadership and commitment to national unity. At a height of 182 meters, it is the tallest statue in the world, surpassing the previous record held by the Spring Temple Buddha in China.

Culture
The Statue of Unity holds immense cultural and historical significance as a symbol of India's unity, strength, and resilience. Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel played a pivotal role in the integration of princely states into the Indian Union following independence, earning him the title "Iron Man of India." The statue serves as a reminder of his visionary leadership and the values of unity, integrity, and diversity that he espoused. It has become a revered pilgrimage site for Indians and a source of national pride, inspiring admiration and reverence for Sardar Patel's contributions to the nation.

History
The idea for the Statue of Unity was conceived in 2010 by then Chief Minister of Gujarat, Narendra Modi, who envisioned a grand monument to honor Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel's legacy. Construction of the statue began in 2013 and was completed in 2018, coinciding with Sardar Patel's 143rd birth anniversary. The statue was inaugurated by Prime Minister Narendra Modi in a grand ceremony attended by dignitaries from across the country. Since then, the Statue of Unity has emerged as a major tourist attraction, drawing visitors from around the world to marvel at its grandeur and pay homage to the Iron Man of India.
